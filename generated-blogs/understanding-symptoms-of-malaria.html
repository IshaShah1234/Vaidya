Recognizing the Symptoms of Malaria

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites. These parasites are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Early detection of malaria symptoms can lead to timely treatment and potentially save lives.

Common Symptoms of Malaria

The symptoms of malaria typically develop within 10 days to 4 weeks following the infection. In some cases, symptoms may not appear until several months after exposure. Common symptoms include: high fever, profuse sweating, chills, headache, vomiting, diarrhea, and fatigue.

Severe Malaria Symptoms

In severe cases, malaria can cause serious complications such as: severe anemia due to destruction of red blood cells, cerebral malaria with abnormal behavior, seizures, or coma, and kidney failure or pulmonary edema which can lead to breathing difficulties.

Recognizing Malaria in Children

In children, malaria symptoms may also manifest as severe malnutrition or respiratory distress (trouble breathing), in addition to the aforementioned symptoms.

When to Seek Medical Attention

If you or a loved one are experiencing any of the symptoms of malaria, especially if you have recently travelled to a region where malaria is prevalent, it is crucial to seek medical attention immediately.

FAQs About Malaria Symptoms

Can malaria symptoms be mild?

Yes, in some cases, individuals infected with malaria may experience mild symptoms, which can often be mistaken for a flu or viral infection.

What is the incubation period for malaria?

The incubation period - the time between being bitten by an infected mosquito and the appearance of symptoms - varies depending on the type of Plasmodium parasite. It typically ranges from 7 to 30 days.

Can you have malaria without fever?

While fever is a common symptom of malaria, it is possible to have malaria without a fever, especially in the early stages of the disease.

Understanding the Symptoms of Dengue

Dengue, a mosquito-borne tropical disease, affects millions of people worldwide every year. Early detection and medical intervention can significantly reduce risks associated with this disease. Let's discuss the symptoms of dengue.

Early Symptoms of Dengue

During the early stages of dengue, symptoms can be mild and are often mistaken for other illnesses like the flu. These include:

  • High fever
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Swollen glands
  • Rash
  • Nausea and vomiting

Severe Dengue

In some cases, symptoms can escalate into severe dengue or dengue hemorrhagic fever. Warning signs of severe dengue usually appear 3-7 days after the first symptoms and include:

  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting
  • Bleeding gums or nose
  • Blood in urine, stools, or vomit
  • Rapid breathing
  • Fatigue and restlessness

When to Seek Medical Help

If you or someone you know exhibits these symptoms, particularly after traveling to a dengue-prone area, seek immediate medical help. Early diagnosis and treatment can make a significant difference in recovery.

FAQs About Dengue Symptoms

Q: How quickly do dengue symptoms appear after a mosquito bite?
A: Symptoms typically appear 4-7 days after being bitten by a mosquito carrying the dengue virus.

Q: Can dengue symptoms reappear?
A: Yes, in some cases, a person can recover and then symptoms can reappear and become more severe.

Q: How long do dengue symptoms last?
A: Dengue fever usually lasts for 2-7 days. In severe cases, symptoms can persist for more than a week.

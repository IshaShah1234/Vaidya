Understanding the Symptoms of Typhoid Fever

Typhoid fever is a potentially life-threatening illness caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi. Knowing its symptoms can lead to early diagnosis and treatment, reducing the risk of complications.

Early Signs and Symptoms

Early symptoms usually appear 1-2 weeks after exposure and may include: high fever, headache, abdominal pain, constipation or diarrhea, and rose spots on the skin.

Progressive Symptoms

As the disease progresses, it may cause severe complications like intestinal bleeding or perforation. Symptoms include persistent high fever, fatigue, weight loss, and confusion.

When to Seek Medical Care

Immediate medical attention should be sought if one experiences persistent high fever, severe abdominal pain, or signs of confusion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is typhoid contagious?

Yes, typhoid is contagious and is primarily spread through contaminated food or water.

How can typhoid be prevented?

Good hygiene practices and typhoid vaccination can help prevent the disease.

Can typhoid fever be cured?

Yes, with early diagnosis and appropriate antibiotic treatment, typhoid fever can be cured.

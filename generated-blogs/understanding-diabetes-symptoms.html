Symptoms of Diabetes

People with diabetes often exhibit a range of symptoms. These may include:

  • Increased thirst and hunger
  • Frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
  • Tingling hands and feet

FAQs about Diabetes Symptoms

  • What are the early signs of diabetes?

    The early signs of diabetes often include increased thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss.

  • How can diabetes be diagnosed?

    Diabetes is diagnosed through blood tests that measure your blood sugar levels.

  • Can diabetes symptoms be reversed?

    While diabetes itself cannot be reversed, early treatment and lifestyle changes can help manage symptoms and prevent complications.

  • Are the symptoms of diabetes different in men and women?

    While most symptoms are the same in both sexes, women may also experience gestational diabetes during pregnancy.

  • Is fatigue a symptom of diabetes?

    Yes, chronic fatigue can be a symptom of diabetes as your body isn't able to effectively use glucose for energy.

  • Can diabetes lead to vision problems?

    Yes, diabetes can cause a range of eye problems, including blurred vision and, in severe cases, blindness.

  • What is the link between diabetes and slow-healing sores?

    Diabetes can affect your body's ability to heal wounds, leading to slow-healing sores, particularly on the feet.

  • Can frequent infections be a sign of diabetes?

    Yes, frequent infections can indicate a weakened immune system, which may be a sign of diabetes.

Related Conditions

Diabetes often coexists with other conditions like hypertension, heart disease, and obesity. It's crucial to manage these conditions to effectively control diabetes.
